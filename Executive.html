Executive Summary. Project 19859D is a deployable, autonomous urban district designed for net-zero operation, mixed-use density, and long-term asset value. This document provides the complete technical, economic, and operational specification for qualified investors, municipalities, developers, and institutional partners.
01 — PRODUCT SPECIFICATIONS

The Urban Product

19859D is not a building. It is an integrated urban district designed for replicability, operational efficiency, and resident quality of life.

DISTRICT MODEL
15‑Minute City
All daily needs — work, education, health, retail, leisure, mobility — reachable within 15 minutes of walking or rolling. No car dependency.
RESIDENTIAL OFFERING
Mid‑rise, high‑amenity
Buildings from 4 to 7 floors. Unit sizes: 45m² to 140m². Private outdoor space for every unit. Ground-floor retail and services integrated.
MIXED‑USE PROGRAM
Residential + commercial + R&D
65% residential, 20% commercial/retail, 10% research & production, 5% civic and cultural. Balanced, resilient revenue mix.
DENSITY TARGET
180 residents/hectare
Comparable to healthy European urban density (Vienna, Copenhagen). Enough for vitality, not enough for congestion.
UNIT MIX
Diverse typologies
1-bed (35%), 2-bed (40%), 3-bed (20%), 4-bed (5%). All units include private balcony or terrace. Minimum ceiling height: 2.8m.
AMENITIES
District‑scale services
Shared workspaces, fitness centers, childcare facilities, community gardens, medical clinic, and cultural spaces integrated across the district.

04 — DEPLOYMENT TIMELINE

Phased Delivery

Three-phase deployment model from site preparation to full operational autonomy.

2026–27
PHASE 0 — SITE & INFRASTRUCTURE
Geological surveys, platform stabilization, underground logistics tunnel (8km), deep aquifer drilling (12 wells), PV foundation installation. Workforce: 4,200.
2027–29
PHASE 1 — ENERGY & WATER SYSTEMS
PV array Phase 1 (420 MWp) online, BESS commissioning, CSP tower erection, RO plant operational, AWG array deployed. NEXUS testing begins.
2029–31
PHASE 2 — URBAN CONSTRUCTION
Residential clusters R1–R3 (3,800 units), civic nucleus, transit system, vertical farming towers. First 4,000 residents for operational testing.
2031
PHASE 3 — FULL OCCUPATION
12,000 residents, full autonomous operation, closed-loop systems online. External audit. Phase II design review (target: 28,000 by 2038).
05 — GOVERNANCE & SOCIAL MODEL

Operational Framework

A progressive governance model designed for stability, participation, and long-term resident alignment.

GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE
Liquid democracy
Daily operations managed by elected Sector Councils (12 members per zone, 2-year terms). Strategic decisions require 67% supermajority via digital referendum. No permanent administrative class. All senior roles term-limited to 4 years, non-renewable.
RESIDENT ENDOWMENT
Universal City Endowment
Housing (80–120m² tiered by household size), basic food allocation, healthcare, education, and transit — included in residency. Funded by R&D zone productivity revenue.
POPULATION PROFILE
12,000 residents, 62 nationalities
28% research/science, 24% technical operations, 18% services, 14% arts/culture, 10% entrepreneurship, 6% governance. 40% under 35.
Age quota under 35Mandatory 40%
DATA SOVEREIGNTY
NEXUS privacy architecture
All personal data processed locally on resident devices (edge AI). NEXUS receives only anonymized, aggregated data. No facial recognition. Independent Resident Data Council.
