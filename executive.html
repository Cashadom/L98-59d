01 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A New Class of Urban Asset

Project 19859D is not a building. It is a fully integrated, deployable urban district model designed for net-zero operation, mixed-use density, long-term asset value and replication across metropolitan contexts.

19859D responds to three structural urban crises simultaneously: the housing affordability gap, energy dependency, and climate vulnerability. It does so through integrated closed-loop systems — not through isolated building-scale optimizations — producing verifiable, certification-backed performance at district scale.
District model
15-Minute City
All daily needs — work, education, health, retail, leisure, mobility — reachable within 15 minutes on foot or by shared autonomous pod. Zero car dependency.
Asset class
Investment-Grade ESG
Structured as a bankable urban product. Diversified revenue streams, 6.5–7.5% stabilized yield, BREEAM / LEED Platinum target. EU Taxonomy aligned.
Scalability
Replicable Model
Modular framework adaptable to arid, temperate and tropical climates. Scalable from 5,000 to 50,000 residents. Phase II target: 28,000 residents by 2038.

03 — INTEGRATED TECHNICAL SYSTEMS

Infrastructure & Closed-Loop Systems

Every technical choice is driven by measurable performance, long-term cost efficiency, and contribution to closed material and energy loops.

ENERGY SYSTEM

Primary generation
Bifacial PV + CSP
847 MWp bifacial monocrystalline (28.4% efficiency) + 140 MWe concentrated solar power with 14h molten salt storage at 560°C. Round-trip efficiency: 93%.
Storage & dispatch
LFP BESS + AI
2.4 GWh cobalt-free LFP battery system. 4,000-cycle lifespan. AI-managed dispatch with 24h solar forecast (±3% accuracy). 18-hour full autonomy.

WATER SYSTEM

Source 1 — Aquifer
Deep drilling
12,000 m³/day from 480m deep aquifer via reverse osmosis. 12 wells. Energy cost: 3.2 kWh/m³. Hydrogeological study required (BRGM).
Source 2 — AWG
Atmospheric water
1,400 MOF-801 based atmospheric water generation units. 140,000 L/day. Powered by district's renewable surplus.
Recycling loop
94% reuse
Greywater recycling for irrigation, cooling, sanitation. Zero liquid discharge achieved by year 3 of operation. 85 L/person/day potable consumption.

FOOD SYSTEM

Vertical farming
36 towers — 64,800 m²
Aeroponic systems. 94% less water than field agriculture. 420 tonnes/year of fresh produce. Crops: leafy greens, herbs, tomatoes, peppers, strawberries.
Closed-loop protein
Aquaponics + BSF
38 tonnes/year tilapia (aquaponics). Black Soldier Fly larvae facility: 12 tonnes/month protein + 8 tonnes/month organic fertilizer. 65% caloric self-sufficiency by 2035.

MOBILITY — 3-TIER SYSTEM

Tier 1 — Transit
Autonomous BRT
6 lines, 48 vehicles, 90s peak headways. Average wait: 2.8 minutes. 80 passengers/vehicle.
Tier 2 — Local
Micro-mobility pods
2,400 autonomous 2-person pods. Demand-responsive. Average deployment: 4.2 min. 840 integrated charging nodes.
Tier 3 — Freight
Underground logistics
22 km of 3.4m tunnels at −8m. Zero surface freight traffic. 3 perimeter interface nodes. Fully automated.

NEXUS AI PLATFORM

Data infrastructure
4.2B data points/day
380,000 IoT nodes across 14 subsystems. Full digital twin for all critical assets. Predictive maintenance: 96h advance warning for 94% of failures. 38% labor reduction vs conventional systems.
Privacy architecture
Edge AI — No surveillance
All personal data processed locally on resident devices. NEXUS receives only anonymized aggregated data. No facial recognition. No individual tracking. Independent Resident Data Council.

06 — GOVERNANCE & SOCIAL MODEL

Operational Framework

A progressive governance model designed for long-term stability, active participation, and resident alignment — without a permanent administrative class.

Governance structure
Liquid Democracy
Elected Sector Councils (12 members per zone, 2-year terms). Strategic decisions require 67% supermajority via digital referendum. All senior roles term-limited to 4 years, non-renewable.
Resident endowment
Universal City Endowment
Housing (80–120 m² by household size), basic food allocation, healthcare, education, and transit — included in residency. Funded by R&D zone productivity revenue.
Population profile
12,000 residents — 62 nationalities
28% research/science · 24% technical operations · 18% services · 14% arts/culture · 10% entrepreneurship · 6% governance. 40% under 35 — structural objective.
Data sovereignty
NEXUS Privacy Architecture
All personal data processed locally on resident devices (edge AI). NEXUS receives only anonymized, aggregated data. No facial recognition. Independent Resident Data Council. GDPR compliant by design.
