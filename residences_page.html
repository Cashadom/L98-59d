A residential offer designed as part of the district

These homes are not isolated real-estate units. They are part of a coherent neighborhood system where architecture, mobility, services, energy performance, and public space reinforce one another. The result is a calmer form of urban ownership: lower daily friction, more usable space, and stronger long-term desirability.

Structuralist, ecological, modern-futurist
The residential language remains disciplined and legible: clear geometry, strong horizontals, honest materials, generous glazing, planted terraces, and durable mid-rise proportions. Environmental logic is embedded in the architecture itself through orientation, passive cooling, shaded circulation, and efficient envelopes.

02 — Compact ownership typology

URBAN STUDIO

A compact residence designed for first-time owners, young professionals, couples, or a highly efficient pied-à-terre. The ambition is not minimum space but maximum usefulness: clean structural grids, integrated storage, deep balconies, generous light, and immediate access to work, retail, wellness, and public space.

45–55 m²Indicative internal area
1–2Residents
BalconyPrivate outdoor extension
EfficientCompact urban ownership

Designed for calm density

The Urban Studio brings premium essentials into a smaller footprint: strong ceiling lines, warm industrial finishes, controlled sunlight, durable kitchens, and a layout that avoids wasted circulation.

  • Open living / kitchen zone with clear sightlines
  • Integrated storage and utility wall
  • Balcony or recessed terrace extension
  • Large-format glazing with solar control
  • Suitable for first ownership or long-stay urban use
  • Direct access to district services within minutes

03 — Long-term family typology

FAMILY RESIDENCE

The central residential product of the district: a flexible home for households seeking long-term livability, generous shared space, and practical proximity to schools, health services, green areas, and daily retail. The family residence balances warmth and discipline — more terrace, more storage, more adaptability.

85–110 m²Indicative internal area
2–3 bedsHousehold-ready layout
Large terraceOutdoor family extension
FlexibleLong-term liveability

The everyday family product

This typology is shaped around ordinary usefulness: larger dining space, comfortable bedroom separation, adaptable secondary rooms, protected outdoor areas, and durable interiors that age well with family life.

  • Generous living / dining area with terrace continuity
  • Two or three-bedroom configurations
  • Family kitchen with strong functional zoning
  • Deep balconies or terraces for outdoor use
  • Storage, utility, and circulation designed for real life
  • Ideal for resident-owners planning long stays

04 — Larger premium typology

SIGNATURE RESIDENCE

The most expansive residential format shown on this page. Positioned for households seeking more volume, more privacy, stronger views, and a more elevated ownership experience within the same low-carbon district logic. Premium here means more space and more calm — not excess for its own sake.

120–140 m²Indicative internal area
3–4 bedsLarger family capacity
Corner / dual aspectLight & orientation
PremiumExpanded outdoor living

More volume, same district coherence

Signature residences extend the architectural language into larger domestic space: wider terraces, stronger daylight quality, more generous master suites, and layouts suited to family, hosting, and long-duration occupation.

  • Expanded living areas with stronger indoor-outdoor flow
  • Multiple-bedroom family configuration
  • Corner or dual-aspect daylight strategy
  • Premium terrace positioning and broader views
  • Higher privacy with stable long-term value
  • Flagship Phase I ownership product
